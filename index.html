Ball Python Care

A simple yet comprehensive guide

Struggling? You're not the only one!

Picture this: you've just arrived home with a new, wondeful pet: your first ball python!

Excitedly, you sit the ball python in the terrarium you so proudly designed. For the next few days, weeks, possibly even months, everything goes fine. Lil' Noodle, or whatever you've called them, looks like they're having the time of their lives. Slithering around, exploring new places, eating delicious mice (or any other rodents); they're literally in heaven.

Then it stops. You don't know why it stops, it just stops. Lil' Noodle stops eating, or perhaps they suddenly become more aggressive, or maybe none of those. All you know is that something is wrong.

Overtime, you try everything you can to help poor Lil' Noodle, but nothing seems to work. As days turn to weeks, and weeks turn to months, you find yourself wondering whether you are a good owner; perhaps you're even considering giving Lil' Noodle away to a better home...

Relax!

The above scenario, whilst written somewhat apocalyptically, is actually quite common. Luckily, this website is here to help. I made this website after experiencing some problems with my own baby ball python, "Noodle Doodle". After weeks, if not months, of researching both online and offline, I've compiled everything I've learnt here into many different sections. Below is an overview of each section.
